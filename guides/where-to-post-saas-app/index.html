Where to Post Your SaaS App

Not all distribution channels are equal. Some produce vanity metrics. Some produce paying customers. Here is an honest ranking of where to post your SaaS app and what to expect from each.

Every week, solo founders launch SaaS products on every channel simultaneously and wonder why they have 200 signups and 0 paying customers. The problem is usually channel selection — not all traffic converts equally, and some channels produce almost no paying customers at any volume.

The channels ranked by customer quality

1. Direct outreach — highest quality, lowest volume. A message to someone who has the exact problem produces the highest conversion rate of any channel. Slow to scale, but the feedback quality is irreplaceable at early stage.

2. Niche communities (Reddit, Slack, Discord) — medium quality, medium volume. Conversion depends entirely on how relevant the community is to your specific customer. A post in the right subreddit outperforms a Product Hunt launch for many micro SaaS products.

3. Indie Hackers — medium quality, medium volume. The audience is founders, which is your target if you're building for founders. Less relevant if you're building for a different audience.

4. Product Hunt — low conversion, high visibility. Good for social proof and SEO backlinks. Poor for direct customer acquisition unless your product targets the PH audience specifically (which is mostly other founders and early adopters).

5. Twitter/X — highly variable. A founder with an existing audience can drive significant signups. A founder starting from zero will see minimal traction. Build the audience before you need it.

6. SEO — zero short-term return, highest long-term ROI. A well-ranked SEO page drives inbound traffic indefinitely. Takes 6–12 months to produce results.

Product Hunt — when and how

Launch on Product Hunt when you have: real testimonials with real names and products, a polished landing page with clear positioning, and at least 10 paying customers to reference. Not before.

The PH launch checklist that matters: launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday, have 20+ people ready to upvote and comment on launch day, respond to every comment personally within the first hour, and have a launch-day offer that creates urgency.

Indie Hackers

The Indie Hackers launch post works when it tells a genuine story. What problem did you have? What did you build? What happened? The posts that perform are honest about the journey — including the parts that didn't work. Pure product promotion is ignored.

Engage in the community for 4–6 weeks before launching. Answer questions. Comment on other people's posts. When you launch, you'll have a reputation rather than just a post.

Reddit and niche communities

Find the subreddit where your target customer complains about the problem your product solves. Read the top posts from the last year. Post when you have something genuinely useful to contribute — a solution to a specific problem, a real result, a lesson learned. Not a product announcement.

The posts that drive signups from Reddit are almost always framed as "I had this problem, I built a solution, here's what I learned" — not "I built a product, check it out."

Direct outreach — the underrated channel

Direct outreach to people who have publicly described the problem your product solves is the highest-converting acquisition channel available to a solo founder. LinkedIn, Twitter, forum posts — anyone who has written about the pain you solve is a warm prospect.

The message that works: reference their specific situation, describe the problem in their language, and offer to show them something that solves it. Not a pitch — an offer to solve a specific problem you saw them describe.

SEO — the long game

Start your SEO content the day you launch. Not to drive traffic this week — to build the foundation for inbound traffic 6–12 months from now. The solo founder who publishes one well-targeted SEO page per week for 6 months will have a meaningful organic channel by month 7. The one who waits until they "have time" never gets there.
