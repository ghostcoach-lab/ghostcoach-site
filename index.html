The problem

You're probably here because
one of these is true.

The product isn't the hard part. Every one of these problems is a business problem — not a product problem.

You launched and got signups. Nobody paid.
800 upvotes. 2 paying customers. You feel like you failed. You didn't — but Product Hunt is not an acquisition system. You just don't have one yet.
Your MRR is flat and you don't know why.
Growth happened fast for two months. Then it stopped. You're spending more time refreshing Stripe than fixing the actual problem — which is almost always acquisition or churn.
You're charging too little and you know it.
$9/mo. $12/mo. Maybe $19. You're scared to raise prices because you think you'll lose everyone. That fear is costing you more than a price increase ever would.
Your churn is killing growth faster than you can fill the bucket.
New signups. Same MRR. The math doesn't add up until you realise you're losing two thirds of your customers every quarter. Fixing a leaky bucket by filling it faster is not a strategy.

FAQ

Questions worth asking
before you commit.

How is this different from just using ChatGPT or Claude? +
ChatGPT and Claude are general-purpose tools — they give you a balanced overview of whatever you ask. GhostCoach is built for one specific job: helping solopreneurs turn a subscription product into a revenue-generating business. Marcus has an opinionated framework built from decades of documented expertise from SaaS founders and operators — not just AI training data. It knows your specific situation and gives you one recommendation, not a menu of options. The difference is between asking someone who has seen your exact problem fifty times before, and searching the internet.
I already have Claude Pro. Is GhostCoach just a wrapper? +
GhostCoach is built on the Anthropic API — same underlying model as Claude. What differs is the Ghost OS framework: years of expertise from SaaS founders, operators, and advisors who have already made the mistakes you're trying to avoid, encoded into every session.

Is Marcus a real person? +
Marcus is an AI coaching system built on real business strategy frameworks developed from working with 50+ solopreneur product businesses. It will not pretend to be human. What it will do is give you more specific, more actionable advice than most human coaches would — because it has no incentive to keep the conversation going longer than it needs to be.
What stage of business is GhostCoach built for? +
Three stages: pre-launch (getting pricing and positioning right before you go live), just launched (first users, working toward first revenue), and growing — which includes both founders scaling consistently and founders who have hit a plateau and are stuck. GhostCoach is built for solopreneurs who want to grow without hiring — there is no upper MRR limit as long as you are still running the business solo.
What kind of products does GhostCoach work for? +
Subscription-based digital products — SaaS tools, apps, and recurring-revenue software. The five business pillars are built around subscription mechanics: pricing, trial-to-paid conversion, churn, and acquisition. If you sell one-time purchase products or downloadables, the advice will still be useful but the framework is not optimised for your model.
How does the memory work? +
At the start of every session, Marcus receives your full profile — what you've built, your current stage, your tools, your bottleneck, your 90-day goal — and a rolling summary of your last four sessions. This means Marcus can reference what was discussed previously, what you committed to doing, and whether things have changed. After each session, a summary is automatically generated and stored. On the Builder plan and above, this memory is active in every session.
What is the weekly progress digest on the Operator plan? +
Every Monday morning, Marcus reviews your recent sessions and sends you three specific actions for the week — derived from what you actually discussed, not a generic checklist.

Your GhostCoach week — Monday 14 April
Based on your last 3 sessions, here are your 3 actions for this week:
1. Raise your annual plan from $229 to $399 this week. Your last session confirmed your churn is happening after month 3 — which means annual customers are staying. Price them accordingly.
2. Email the 7 customers who cancelled in the last 30 days with one question: "Was there a moment where you stopped using it?" Not a survey — one reply-to email, this Tuesday.
3. Hold the content channel for 3 more weeks before evaluating. You've published 5 posts. That's not enough signal. Don't make a channel decision on 5 data points.
I've bought SaaS courses and read everything. Why will this be different? +
Most courses give you the right framework applied to a generic situation. GhostCoach applies a framework to your specific situation — your product, your churn number, your acquisition channel, your 90-day goal.

The difference shows up in the first question Marcus asks. If you say your churn is 8%, a course tells you about churn. Marcus asks when you're losing people — first 14 days, weeks 2–8, or months 2+? Each window is a different problem with a different fix. The framework doesn't start from first principles. It starts from your numbers.

If you've read everything and it hasn't moved the needle, the issue usually isn't the framework — it's that no framework has been applied specifically to your situation. That's what Marcus does.

Marcus
