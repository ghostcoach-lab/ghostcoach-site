The instinct to start free makes sense — it feels like the lowest-friction path to first users. I want to push back on it though, because free-to-paid migration is consistently harder than it looks.

Before I give you a specific number: what does your product replace — time, money, or another tool? And what does that alternative currently cost your customer? That answer determines the right price — without it, any number I suggest is a guess.

Marcus

Online
You
My churn is around 8% monthly. I've been flat for 6 months. I don't know if it's an onboarding problem, a product gap, or if I'm just attracting the wrong customers.
Marcus
Those are three completely different problems with three different fixes — so the diagnosis matters before we touch anything else.

One question first: when in the customer lifecycle are you losing people? Roughly — first 14 days, weeks 2–8, or after 2+ months of active use?
You
Mostly in the first two weeks I think. After that people tend to stick.
Marcus
That is an onboarding problem, not a product problem. Users are leaving before they reach the moment where your product proves its value.

The fix is not better email sequences. It is engineering one specific moment — the thing a retained user does in session one that a churned user does not — and making it happen for everyone. Do you know what that moment is for your product?
The framework

Every recommendation maps to
one of five pillars.

Marcus built the Ghost OS from working with 50+ solopreneur product businesses. When you bring a problem, Marcus tells you which pillar it belongs to — and sometimes, which pillar the real problem belongs to.

1
Offer Architecture
How your product is packaged, priced, and positioned. Freemium or paid? Monthly or annual? One plan or three? This pillar sets your revenue ceiling before a single customer signs up.
2
AI Delivery Stack
How AI runs your operations — customer onboarding, support, renewals — so you are never the bottleneck in your own product. As a solo operator this gives you leverage that most teams cannot match.
3
Acquisition System
Repeatable, owned channels that compound. Not launch events. A system that brings in strangers every week without paid ads or a sales team.
4
Automation Layer
Billing flows, churn alerts, trial follow-up, onboarding sequences. The connective tissue that lets the business run while you keep building.
5
Revenue Protection
Trial-to-paid conversion, churn reduction, expansion revenue. The highest-leverage growth lever most solo builders ignore until it's too late.
